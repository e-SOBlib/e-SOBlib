Practice of Starting Entrepreneurship Venture

Insights synthesized through the lens of Peter Drucker

"The best way to predict the future is to create it. Entrepreneurship is not a personality trait; it is a clinical discipline."
Peter Drucker

1. Idea to Opportunity Mapping

Not every idea is an opportunity. A scholar's distinction is simple: an idea is a creative thought; an opportunity is a viable, sustainable way to solve a problem for a customer who is willing to pay.

  • The Filter: Look for "incongruities"‚Äîthe gap between what is and what "ought" to be.
  • Feasibility Analysis:
    • Market Fit: Is there a real pain point?
    • Scalability: Can this grow beyond a one-person hobby?

2. Business Modelling

The Business Model is the logic of how your company creates, delivers, and captures value.

The Value Proposition

What specific value do you deliver? (e.g., Is it cheaper, faster, or more prestigious?)

Business Model Canvas

Drucker's Insight: "The purpose of a business is to create a customer." Your model must reflect exactly how you intend to do that.

3. Business Planning

While the model is a sketch, the Business Plan is the blueprint. It is a formal document used to communicate the vision to stakeholders and investors.

Executive Summary: The "elevator pitch" in written form.
Market Analysis: Data-driven proof of market demand.
Operational Plan: Logistics, supply chain, and technology.
Financial Projections: The "Reality Check" (Break-even analysis).

4. Business Creation (Execution)

Turning your validated business concept into a fully operational entity through structured implementation.

1. Legal Structuring
Choosing the appropriate legal entity for your business:
  • Sole Proprietorship: Simplest form, personal liability, full control
  • Partnership: Shared ownership, combined resources, joint liability
  • LLC: Limited liability protection, flexible taxation
  • Corporation (C-Corp/S-Corp): Strong liability protection, easier funding access
Registration Tax ID Licenses
2. Resource Acquisition
Gathering essential resources through strategic planning:
  • Money: Bootstrapping, angel investors, venture capital, loans, crowdfunding
  • Men (Talent): Hiring core team, defining roles, establishing culture
  • Materials: Equipment, technology stack, inventory, office space
  • Intangibles: Intellectual property, partnerships, supplier relationships
Funding Recruitment Procurement
3. Launch (MVP Strategy)
Systematic market entry with measurable outcomes:
  • MVP Definition: Core features solving primary customer pain points
  • Beta Testing: Limited release to early adopters for feedback
  • Data Collection: User behavior, conversion rates, retention metrics
  • Iterative Development: Rapid cycles of feedback ‚Üí improvement ‚Üí relaunch
Validation Feedback Iteration
Execution Timeline & Success Indicators:
Month 1-2: Legal setup & core team assembly
Month 3-4: MVP development & initial funding
Month 5-6: Launch, data collection, pivot/persevere decision
