Network Development Strategies
  • Weak Ties Theory (Granovetter): Casual connections often provide more opportunities than strong ties
  • Structural Holes (Burt): Position yourself between unconnected networks

Networking Metrics

  • Centrality: Your position in the network
  • Relationship Strength: Frequency, intimacy, duration

Innovation for Business Growth

"Innovation is the specific instrument of entrepreneurship." â€” Peter Drucker

Christensen's Innovation Framework

Clayton Christensen's seminal work distinguishes three types of innovation:

Sustaining Innovation

Improves existing products for current customers

  • Incremental improvements
  • Better performance
  • Higher margins

Example: iPhone camera upgrades

Low-End Disruption

Targets overserved customers with simpler, cheaper solutions

  • Lower cost structure
  • Good enough performance
  • New market entrants

Example: Digital cameras vs film

New-Market Disruption

Creates new markets by turning non-consumers into consumers

  • Accessibility focus
  • Simplicity
  • Affordability

Example: Personal computers

Innovation Management Systems
Stage-Gate Process (Cooper)
  1. Discovery: Idea generation
  2. Scoping: Preliminary assessment
  3. Business Case: Detailed investigation
  4. Development: Product design
  5. Testing & Validation
  6. Launch: Commercialization
Lean Startup Methodology (Ries)
  • Build-Measure-Learn: Rapid iteration cycle
  • Minimum Viable Product: Test with minimal features
  • Validated Learning: Data-driven decisions
  • Pivot or Persevere: Strategic direction choices
